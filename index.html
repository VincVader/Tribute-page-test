Napoléon Bonaparte

French military leader who created an empire that stretched across almost all of Europe

Napoleon Bonaparte Timeline:

  • 1769 - Born in Ajaccio, French
  • 1779 - Moved to the French mainland and enrolled at a religious school in Autun then 5 month later he transfered with a scholarship to a military academy at Brienne-le-Château
  • 1784 - On completion of his studies at Brienne Napoleon was admitted to the École Militaire in Paris
  • 1785 - Graduate from a French military academy
  • 1793 - Following a clash with the nationalist Corsican governor, Pasquale Paoli, the Bonaparte family fled their native island for mainland France, where Napoleon returned to military duty
  • 1794 - Was promoted to the rank of brigadier general in the army
  • 1795 - Napoleon helped suppress a royalist insurrection against the revolutionary government in Paris and was promoted to major general
  • 1796 - Married Josephine de Beauharnais
  • 1796 - Napoleon leads the Italian Army in a series of battles against Austria
  • 1804 - Napoleon is named Emperor of the French Empire and, in December, is crowned in a very fancy ceremony at the Notre Dame in Paris. He is named King of Italy a year later
  • 1809 - Napoleon divorces his wife, Josephine, because she had not had any children with him
  • 1810 - Napoleon marries 18 year-old Marie-Louise. She is the daughter of the Emperor of Austria, Francis I.
  • 1811 - The first Napoleon son, Napoleon II, called The King of Rome by Napoleon, is born. He is also called Franz, after his grandfather, Francis I
  • 1814 - After much of Europe unites against Napoleon, his is forced to give up the throne and go to Elba, an island near Italy. His wife and son are forced to leave Paris and they go to Austria to live
  • 1815 - After escaping from Elba, Napoleon goes back to France to try and take control of the army and the government again. The British and Prussians defeat France in June at the Battle of Waterloo
  • 1815 - After his defeat at Waterloo, Napoleon gives up the throne again. This time, he will be sent away to Saint Helena in the South Atlantic, which is much farther away from France than Elba
  • 1821 - While exiled to Saint Helena, Napoleon dies at the age of 51. There are rumors that he is poisoned, but the actual cause of death is most likely stomach cancer
